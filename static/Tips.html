Tips

  • If it looks wrong, it usually is
  • Watch out for the lowest bidder
    • May be more likely to cut corners
    • May use substandard materials
    • May start the job and walk off with your money
    • May start asking for more money to complete the job
  • Check the contractor out, with the Better Business Bureau, Angie's List, Permits Office, etc
  • Get Permits - The easy way to get sound and free advice is contact your local permit office.
  • Keep it Dry - To increase the life of your home look for signs of water intrusion. If you find it quick you can save yourself money
    • Strip and caulk windows and doors once a year.
    • Use quality caulking and read the directions for application and life span
    • Look for rot around window and doors
  • Keep the trees away from your house
  • Having plants next to the house usually looks great, but insure there is adequate drainage away from the foundation wall. Over time the water you use will seep into the concrete and may cause damage
